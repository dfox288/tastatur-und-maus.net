title: "First-time sponsors"
items:
  - q: "What's a demo?"
    a: "A demo is a self-contained computer program — typically 4 megabytes or smaller — that generates real-time audiovisual art: music, 3D scenes, particle systems, shaders, all running live from code with no pre-rendered frames. At Revision, demos compete in timed showings judged by the audience. The technical constraints are the art form: a 4K Intro (4,096 bytes of executable) that runs a full generative landscape is more impressive than a blockbuster film to this audience."
  - q: "Who's actually in the room?"
    a: "A typical mix: staff and senior engineers at games studios and semiconductor companies, tool developers, audio programmers, graphics engineers, indie developers, and a healthy contingent of students who will be working at those companies in three years. About a third of attendees come from outside Germany. Many have been coming since Revision's first edition in 2011."
  - q: "Can we meet people in person?"
    a: "Yes. Sponsors with booth presence (Silver and above) are in the main hall where attendees spend most of their time. The culture is open: people wander, talk shop, and look at whatever is on your table. Hard sells don't work here; having something genuinely interesting to show or talk about does."
  - q: "What does the money actually pay for?"
    a: "See 'Where the money goes' above. Short version: prize pool, venue, stream, and travel grants. No money goes to paid organiser salaries — TUM e.V. is 100% volunteer-run."
